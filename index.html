Who? Where? When was it Discovered?

Beryllium was discovered in 1798 by a french chemsit named Louis Nicolas Vauquelin, who found this element in the oxide form in Beryl and a green-cloured variety of Beryl, Emerald. louis Nicolas Vauquelin discovered Beryllium in Beryl. in 1828, Beryllium was isolated as the metal independently by a German Chemist Friedrich Wöhler and French Chemist Antoine A.B Bussy by the biology of its chloride with potassium.

Properties

Beryllium is a silvery-white metal, It is relatively soft and has a low density. The Atomic Symbol for Beryllium in the Periodic Table is BE and the Atomic Number is 4.

5 Common Uses for Beryllium

  • Beryllium is alloyed with either copper or nickel to create springs,gyroscopes,electrical contacts, etc.
  • Other alloys of Beryllium are used in aircrafts, missiles, satelites, etc.
  • Used in high-speed aircrafts and msillies
  • Used in gears and cogs particularly in the aviation industry.
  • Also used to make X-Rays

10 Intresting Facts

1. Beryllium is a hard metal, but is brittle at room tempreture!
2. Beryllium is two-thirds the density of aluminium.
3. Beryllium is non-magnetic.
4. Beryllium has a sweet taste.
5. Beryllium is the most abundunt element in the earth's crust.
6. Beryllium has six times the specific stiffness of steel, by weight.
7. The Sun in our Solar System has 0.1 parts per billion of Beryllium.
8. The Earth’s crust has between 2 to 6 parts per million of Beryllium.
